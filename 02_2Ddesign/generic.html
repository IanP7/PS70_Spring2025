Website Building!

I did two 75 minute classes on HTML many years ago, so I sat down, got W3schools and YouTube pulled up, strapped in, and hoped for the best. I thought the template that we were given was super ugly (no offense, it was just very barebones and not colorful), but I am super bad at making things look pretty (my sister got all the artistic genes), so I hopped over to HTML5 UP and started with one of their templates. It already had a lot of styling integrated to it already, but it looked like a completely foreign language to me, so I began starting to interpret it.

Despite a few roadblocks where I thought that there was going to be actually zero formatting imported into the various pages I wanted to be styled, this was a relatively straightforward process. I figured out how to link pages to each other and change the parts of the template that I needed to change to make this look and work like a PS70 website. The biggest development was figuring out how to repurpose their gallery containers for my own purposes, put my own images in it, and allow it to link to my new pages. I also learned how to position images next to text in HTML and make them look better 


<article class="style6">

From there, the rest of the customization was relatively straightforward and was largely just filling in the text fields with the relavent information! I am looking forward to the rest of the semester and fine-tuning the website with relavent features as they become pertinent!
